Nutritional Adequacy Figures — A Complete Guide

✦ ❧ ✦

Peraturan Menteri Kesehatan No. 28 Year 2019 establishes Indonesia's updated Nutritional Adequacy Figures (AKG) — the official dietary reference values defining the daily intake of energy, macronutrients, vitamins, and minerals needed by healthy Indonesians across all life stages.

Art. I

Purpose & Legal Background

Why Indonesia updated its nutritional reference standards

"Angka Kecukupan Gizi yang dianjurkan bagi Bangsa Indonesia harus disesuaikan dengan perkembangan ilmu pengetahuan dan teknologi serta kondisi perubahan status gizi dan kesehatan masyarakat."

— Permenkes No. 28 / 2019, Preamble

The Ministry of Health issued Permenkes No. 28 of 2019 to update Indonesia's national dietary reference values, replacing the previous AKG established under Permenkes No. 75 of 2013. This update was driven by advances in nutritional science, changes in the nutritional status of the Indonesian population, and alignment with international dietary reference frameworks.

The AKG (Angka Kecukupan Gizi) — or Recommended Dietary Allowances — are used as the scientific foundation for nutrition policies, health programmes, food product labelling (as % Daily Value / AKG), dietary planning, and public nutrition education across Indonesia.

🔬
Science-Based Update

Revised based on the latest nutritional science, updated population data, and new global dietary reference value methodologies from WHO, FAO, and regional bodies.

📊
Policy Foundation

Provides the scientific basis for all national nutrition programmes, school feeding policies, food fortification standards, and clinical dietary guidelines in Indonesia.

🏷️
Food Label Reference

All Indonesian food products must use AKG 2019 values as the basis for calculating percentage Daily Value (%AKG) on nutrition information panels.

Art. II

What is AKG?

Understanding the Nutritional Adequacy Figure framework
💡
Definition: Angka Kecukupan Gizi (AKG)
AKG is the level of daily intake of essential nutrients considered sufficient to meet the nutritional needs of almost all healthy individuals (97–98%) within a specific population group, classified by age, sex, and physiological status (pregnancy/lactation).

AKG values represent population-level recommendations, not individual prescriptions. They are set at levels that meet the needs of the majority of healthy people — accounting for biological variability between individuals. Someone consuming exactly the AKG level may or may not meet their personal needs.

The AKG framework in Permenkes 28/2019 covers three categories of dietary reference values: recommended intakes (AKG), adequate intakes (Asupan Adekuat), and upper tolerable intake levels (Batas Atas Asupan). Together, these guide both prevention of deficiency and prevention of excessive intake.

Component 1
Angka Kecukupan (AK) — Recommended Intake
The average daily intake level sufficient to meet the nutrient requirements of nearly all (97–98%) healthy individuals in a particular life stage and sex group. Used for dietary planning and assessment at the population level.
Component 2
Asupan Adekuat (AA) — Adequate Intake
Used when evidence is insufficient to establish an AK. Based on observed mean intakes of healthy populations. Indicates a level assumed to be nutritionally adequate.
Component 3
Batas Atas Asupan (BA) — Tolerable Upper Intake
The highest average daily nutrient intake level likely to pose no risk of adverse health effects. Chronic intake above this level increases risk of toxicity or adverse effects.
Component 4
Kebutuhan Rata-Rata (EAR) — Estimated Average Requirement
The daily intake level estimated to meet the needs of exactly 50% of healthy individuals in a life stage and sex group. Used primarily in research and population assessment.

Art. VI

Macronutrients in Detail

Energy, protein, fat, and carbohydrate recommendations
01
Energy — Kebutuhan Energi
▾
Energy requirements are calculated based on basal metabolic rate × physical activity level (PAL). The AKG 2019 uses a PAL of 1.70 (moderate activity) as the standard for adult reference values. Energy values differ by sex, age, and body weight reference values for the Indonesian population.
  • Adult male (19–29 yr): 2,650 kcal/day
  • Adult female (19–29 yr): 2,250 kcal/day
  • Pregnancy adds +180 kcal (1st trim.) to +300 kcal (3rd trim.)
  • Lactation adds +330–400 kcal depending on month
02
Protein — Kebutuhan Protein
▾
Protein AKG values are based on nitrogen balance studies and adjusted for mixed (animal + plant) protein digestibility characteristic of Indonesian diets. The recommendation accounts for the slightly lower bioavailability of plant-based proteins typical in Indonesian food patterns.
  • Infants (0–5 mo): 9 g/day (primarily from breast milk)
  • School-age children: 35–50 g/day
  • Adults: 50–65 g/day
  • Protein contributes 10–15% of total energy intake
03
Fat — Kebutuhan Lemak
▾
Total fat recommendations cover both quality and quantity. AKG 2019 distinguishes between total fat, saturated fat, polyunsaturated fat (omega-3 and omega-6), and trans fat. For adults, fat should provide 20–30% of total energy.
  • Total fat (adult male 19–29 yr): 75 g/day
  • Saturated fat: <10% total energy
  • Trans fat: as low as possible (<1% total energy)
  • Omega-6 to omega-3 ratio: 5:1 recommended
  • AKG 2019 adds specific values for EPA+DHA (first time)
04
Carbohydrate & Fibre — Karbohidrat dan Serat
▾
Carbohydrates are the primary energy source in the Indonesian diet, providing 50–65% of total energy. AKG 2019 provides separate values for total carbohydrates, dietary fibre, and added sugar limits.
  • Adult male (19–29 yr): 430 g/day total carbohydrate
  • Dietary fibre (adult): 28–37 g/day (increased from 2013 values)
  • Added sugar: <10% of total energy (WHO aligned)
  • Whole grains and legumes preferred as carbohydrate sources

Art. VIII

How to Use AKG in Practice

Practical applications of AKG 2019 values
🍽️
Dietary Planning

Use AKG as the target intake when designing daily meal plans for individuals or groups. Aim to meet 100% of AKG across a week rather than exactly each day.

📋
Dietary Assessment

Compare actual food intake data (from 24-hour recall or food records) against AKG values to identify potential nutritional gaps or excesses in a population.

🏫
School Feeding Programmes

Government school meal programmes use AKG to set targets: school meals should contribute a specific percentage (e.g. 30%) of the child's daily AKG requirements.

🏥
Clinical Nutrition

Healthcare providers use AKG as a starting reference for patient dietary counselling, though clinical needs may deviate from population-level recommendations.

🏭
Food Industry

Food manufacturers must calculate and declare %AKG on product labels based on the 2019 reference values (see Food Labelling section below).

📖
Public Education

AKG informs national dietary guidelines, the Isi Piringku campaign, and public nutrition communication programmes targeting Indonesian households.

Art. IX

Food Labelling & %AKG

How AKG is applied on Indonesian food product labels

"The percentage of AKG (%AKG) on nutrition information panels must be calculated using the reference values for an adult consuming 2,150 kcal per day as established in Permenkes 28/2019."

— BPOM Regulation on Nutrition Labelling

Indonesian food product labels must include a Nutrition Information Panel (Informasi Nilai Gizi / ING). The %AKG figure on the label tells consumers what percentage of their recommended daily intake a serving of that food provides.

The reference value used for all food labels is standardised at 2,150 kcal per day — representing the average adult requirement. This standardisation allows consumers to compare products easily, even though individual needs vary.

Art. X

AKG 2013 vs AKG 2019

Key changes and improvements in the 2019 update
🔄
Why AKG Was Updated in 2019
The 2013 AKG covered 32 nutritional components. The 2019 update expanded this to 54 components, reflecting new scientific evidence, changing dietary patterns in Indonesia, and the emerging importance of previously under-studied nutrients.
Change AKG 2013 AKG 2019 Impact
Total nutrients covered 32 components 54 components +22 new
Vitamin D (adults) 15 mcg/day 15 mcg/day (updated BA) Upper limit revised
Omega-3 (EPA+DHA) Not specified 200 mg/day (adults) New addition
Dietary Fibre 25–30 g/day 28–37 g/day Increased
Added Sugar limit Not specified <10% total energy New guidance
Choline Not specified 425–550 mg/day New addition
Water intake 2,300–2,600 ml Updated by life stage Refined
Food label reference 2,000 kcal 2,150 kcal Changed baseline
✅
Major Achievements of AKG 2019

First Indonesian AKG to include specific values for EPA+DHA, choline, fluoride, chromium, and manganese. Also the first to explicitly set added sugar upper limits aligned with WHO recommendations.

📌
What Remained Unchanged

Core protein and energy recommendations for most adult groups remain similar to 2013, reflecting stable scientific consensus. The general structure of life stage groupings is also maintained.
